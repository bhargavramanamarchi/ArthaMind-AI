## Banking in India

### Types of Bank Accounts
| Account Type | Purpose | Interest Rate (Approx.) |
|-------------|---------|------------------------|
| Savings Account | Daily banking, salary | 2.5% – 7% p.a. |
| Current Account | Business transactions | 0% (no interest) |
| Fixed Deposit (FD) | Lump sum investment | 6% – 7.5% p.a. |
| Recurring Deposit (RD) | Monthly fixed savings | 5.5% – 7% p.a. |

### Fixed Deposit (FD)
- Minimum tenure: 7 days | Maximum: 10 years
- Premature withdrawal penalty: 0.5% – 1%
- Senior citizen bonus: +0.25% to +0.50%
- Tax-saving FD: 5-year lock-in, eligible under Section 80C (up to ₹1.5 lakh)
- TDS: 10% if interest exceeds ₹40,000/year (₹50,000 for senior citizens)
- Submit Form 15G/15H to avoid TDS if income is below taxable limit

### RBI Key Rates (Reference)
- Repo Rate: Rate at which RBI lends to commercial banks
- Reverse Repo Rate: Rate at which banks deposit funds with RBI
- CRR: Cash Reserve Ratio — percentage of deposits banks keep with RBI
- SLR: Statutory Liquidity Ratio — percentage of deposits in government securities

### DICGC Insurance
Each depositor in a bank is insured up to ₹5,00,000 by DICGC (Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation) across all deposits in the same bank.

## Insurance

### Types of Insurance

#### 1. Term Life Insurance
- Pure protection plan (no maturity benefit)
- Highest coverage at lowest premium
- Ideal cover: 10-15 times annual income
- Riders: Accidental death, critical illness, waiver of premium
- Key providers: LIC, HDFC Life, ICICI Prudential, Max Life

#### 2. Health Insurance
- Covers hospitalization expenses
- Types: Individual, Family Floater, Top-up, Super Top-up
- Waiting period: Pre-existing diseases (2-4 years), specific diseases (1-2 years)
- Tax benefit: Section 80D
  - Self/family: Up to ₹25,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens)
  - Parents: Additional ₹25,000 (₹50,000 if parents are senior citizens)
- Key features to check: Room rent limit, co-payment, sub-limits, network hospitals

#### 3. Motor Insurance
- Third-party: Mandatory by law (covers damage to third party)
- Comprehensive: Covers own damage + third party
- Add-ons: Zero depreciation, engine protection, roadside assistance
- No-claim bonus: 20-50% discount for claim-free years

#### 4. ULIP (Unit Linked Insurance Plan)
- Combines insurance and investment
- Lock-in: 5 years
- Higher charges than mutual funds
- Not recommended for pure investment (mutual funds are better)

### Insurance Claim Process
1. Inform the insurance company immediately
2. Submit claim form with required documents
3. Cashless: Treatment at network hospital (insurer pays directly)
4. Reimbursement: Pay first, submit bills for reimbursement
5. Claim settlement within 30 days of receiving all documents

## Credit Score (CIBIL Score)

### What is CIBIL Score?
CIBIL (TransUnion CIBIL) score ranges from 300 to 900. A score above 750 is considered good.

### Score Ranges
| Score Range | Rating | Loan Approval Chances |
|-------------|--------|----------------------|
| 800-900 | Excellent | Very high, best interest rates |
| 750-799 | Good | High, favorable terms |
| 700-749 | Fair | Moderate, may face higher rates |
| 650-699 | Below Average | Low, may require collateral |
| Below 650 | Poor | Very low, may be rejected |

### How to Improve CIBIL Score
1. Pay all EMIs and credit card bills on time
2. Keep credit utilization below 30%
3. Maintain a healthy mix of secured and unsecured loans
4. Don't apply for multiple loans/cards simultaneously
5. Check your credit report regularly for errors
6. Don't close old credit cards (they improve credit history length)
7. Avoid being a loan guarantor unless necessary

### How to Check CIBIL Score
- Free once a year from cibil.com
- Many banks and apps provide free monthly scores (HDFC, Paytm, CRED)

## UPI & Digital Payments

### UPI (Unified Payments Interface)
- Developed by: NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India)
- Launched: April 2016
- Available 24/7, including holidays
- Transaction limit: ₹1,00,000 per transaction (₹2,00,000 for some banks, ₹5,00,000 for tax payments)
- Daily limit: Varies by bank (typically ₹1,00,000 – ₹2,00,000)

### UPI Features
- Instant money transfer (real-time)
- Virtual Payment Address (VPA): yourname@bankcode
- QR code payments
- Recurring payments (UPI Autopay)
- UPI Lite: For small transactions (up to ₹500, offline capable)
- UPI 123PAY: For feature phones (IVR-based, no internet needed)

### Popular UPI Apps
- BHIM (Government), PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm, Amazon Pay
- Bank apps: YONO (SBI), iMobile (ICICI), PayZapp (HDFC)

### UPI Safety Tips
1. Never share UPI PIN with anyone
2. Don't click unknown payment request links
3. Verify receiver details before sending money
4. Report fraud within 3 days to bank and cybercrime portal
5. Use app lock and phone lock
6. Don't scan QR codes to RECEIVE money (QR codes are for PAYING)

### Other Digital Payment Methods
| Method | Use Case | Limit |
|--------|----------|-------|
| NEFT | Bank transfer (batch processing) | No upper limit |
| RTGS | Large transfers (real-time) | Minimum ₹2,00,000 |
| IMPS | Instant transfer | Up to ₹5,00,000 |
| Net Banking | Online banking portal | Bank-specific |
| Wallets | Small transactions | ₹10,000 (KYC: ₹2,00,000) |

## Emergency Fund

### What is an Emergency Fund?
A liquid reserve of money set aside for unexpected expenses or financial emergencies.

### How Much to Save?
- **Single earner, family**: 6-12 months of expenses
- **Dual earner, no dependents**: 3-6 months of expenses
- **Self-employed / Freelancer**: 9-12 months of expenses

### Where to Keep Emergency Fund?
1. **Savings Account**: Immediate access (low interest)
2. **Liquid Mutual Fund**: 1-day redemption, better returns than savings
3. **FD with premature withdrawal**: Penalty but accessible
4. **Sweep-in FD**: Auto-transfer between savings and FD

### Tips for Building Emergency Fund
- Start with 1 month's expenses, then build up
- Automate monthly transfers to emergency fund
- Keep emergency fund separate from regular savings
- Don't invest emergency fund in equity or locked instruments
- Replenish immediately after using
