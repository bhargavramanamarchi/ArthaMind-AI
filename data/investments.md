## What are Mutual Funds?

A mutual fund is a professionally managed investment vehicle that pools money from multiple investors to purchase a diversified portfolio of securities (stocks, bonds, etc.). In India, mutual funds are regulated by SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India).

## Types of Mutual Funds

### By Asset Class
| Type | Description | Risk Level | Ideal For |
|------|-------------|------------|-----------|
| Equity Funds | Invest primarily in stocks | High | Long-term growth (5+ years) |
| Debt Funds | Invest in bonds, government securities | Low-Medium | Stable returns, short-medium term |
| Hybrid Funds | Mix of equity and debt | Medium | Balanced growth |
| Index Funds | Track a market index (Nifty 50, Sensex) | Medium-High | Passive investors |
| ELSS | Equity with 3-year lock-in, tax saving | High | Tax saving under 80C |

### By Investment Style
- **Large Cap**: Top 100 companies by market cap (lower risk)
- **Mid Cap**: 101st to 250th companies (medium risk)
- **Small Cap**: 251st and beyond (higher risk, higher potential)
- **Multi-Cap**: Invest across all market caps
- **Flexi-Cap**: Fund manager decides allocation

## SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

SIP allows investing a fixed amount regularly (monthly/weekly) in mutual funds.

### Benefits of SIP
- **Rupee Cost Averaging**: Buy more units when NAV is low, fewer when high
- **Power of Compounding**: Returns generate further returns over time
- **Discipline**: Automated investing removes emotion
- **Flexibility**: Start with as low as ₹100/month
- **No Timing Required**: Don't need to predict market movements

### SIP Example
Monthly SIP: ₹5,000 | Return: 12% p.a. | Duration: 20 years
- Total invested: ₹12,00,000
- Expected corpus: ₹49,95,740
- Wealth gained: ₹37,95,740

## NAV (Net Asset Value)

NAV is the per-unit market value of a mutual fund.
NAV = (Total Assets – Total Liabilities) / Number of Units Outstanding

NAV is calculated at the end of each business day.

## Expense Ratio

The annual fee charged by the fund house for managing the fund.
- Regular plans: 1% – 2.5%
- Direct plans: 0.1% – 1% (no distributor commission)
- SEBI maximum limit: 2.25% for equity, 2% for debt (for regular plans)

Always prefer Direct plans for lower costs over time.

## Exit Load

A fee charged when you redeem units before a specified period.
- Equity funds: Typically 1% if redeemed within 1 year
- ELSS: No exit load (but 3-year mandatory lock-in)
- Debt funds: Varies, usually 0-1%
- Index funds: Usually nil or very low

## Mutual Fund Taxation (FY 2025-26)

### Equity Funds
| Holding Period | Tax Type | Rate |
|---------------|----------|------|
| < 12 months | STCG | 20% |
| ≥ 12 months | LTCG | 12.5% (exempt up to ₹1.25 lakh) |

### Debt Funds (purchased after April 1, 2023)
- Gains are added to income and taxed at slab rate
- No indexation benefit

### Hybrid Funds
- If equity component ≥ 65%: Taxed as equity fund
- If equity component < 65%: Taxed as debt fund

## PPF (Public Provident Fund)

- **Interest Rate**: 7.1% p.a. (compounded annually, reviewed quarterly)
- **Lock-in**: 15 years (partial withdrawal allowed from 7th year)
- **Annual Limit**: ₹500 (min) to ₹1,50,000 (max)
- **Tax Benefit**: EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) — investment, interest, and maturity all tax-free
- **Section**: 80C deduction
- **Where to open**: Post office or nationalized banks

## NPS (National Pension System)

- **Regulated by**: PFRDA (Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority)
- **Account Types**: Tier-I (mandatory, retirement) and Tier-II (voluntary, flexible)
- **Asset Classes**: E (Equity), C (Corporate Bonds), G (Government Securities), A (Alternative)
- **Tax Benefits**:
  - Section 80CCD(1): Up to ₹1,50,000 (within 80C limit)
  - Section 80CCD(1B): Additional ₹50,000 (exclusive, over and above 80C)
  - Section 80CCD(2): Employer contribution (up to 14% for government, 10% for others)
- **Withdrawal**: At 60, minimum 40% must be used to buy annuity; 60% can be withdrawn tax-free
- **Lock-in**: Until age 60 (partial withdrawal allowed for specific purposes)

## EPF (Employee Provident Fund)

- **Contribution**: 12% of basic salary by both employee and employer
- **Interest Rate**: 8.25% p.a. (FY 2023-24, declared by EPFO)
- **Tax-free interest**: Up to ₹2.5 lakh annual contribution (excess interest taxable)
- **Withdrawal**: Allowed after 2 months of unemployment or retirement at 58
- **Section 80C**: Employee contribution eligible for deduction

## Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB)

- **Issued by**: RBI on behalf of Government of India
- **Denomination**: In grams of gold (minimum 1 gram)
- **Interest**: 2.5% p.a. (semi-annual, on issue price)
- **Tenure**: 8 years (exit option from 5th year)
- **Tax**: Capital gains tax-exempt if held till maturity
- **Maximum**: 4 kg per individual per financial year
- **Advantage**: No making charges, no storage cost, guaranteed gold price return
