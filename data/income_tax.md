## Income Tax Slabs — New Regime (Default from FY 2023-24)

The New Tax Regime is the default regime from FY 2023-24 onwards. Taxpayers can opt for the Old Regime at the time of filing ITR.

### New Regime Tax Slabs (FY 2025-26 / AY 2026-27)

| Income Slab (₹)          | Tax Rate |
|---------------------------|----------|
| Up to 4,00,000            | Nil      |
| 4,00,001 – 8,00,000      | 5%       |
| 8,00,001 – 12,00,000     | 10%      |
| 12,00,001 – 16,00,000    | 15%      |
| 16,00,001 – 20,00,000    | 20%      |
| 20,00,001 – 24,00,000    | 25%      |
| Above 24,00,000           | 30%      |

Standard Deduction: ₹75,000 for salaried individuals under the New Regime.
Rebate under Section 87A: Full tax rebate for income up to ₹12,00,000 (effective zero tax up to ₹12,75,000 for salaried).

### Old Regime Tax Slabs (FY 2025-26)

| Income Slab (₹)          | Tax Rate |
|---------------------------|----------|
| Up to 2,50,000            | Nil      |
| 2,50,001 – 5,00,000      | 5%       |
| 5,00,001 – 10,00,000     | 20%      |
| Above 10,00,000           | 30%      |

The Old Regime allows deductions under Section 80C (up to ₹1,50,000), Section 80D (health insurance), HRA exemption, LTA, and other exemptions.

## Section 80C Deductions (Old Regime Only)

Maximum deduction: ₹1,50,000 per financial year.

Eligible investments:
- PPF (Public Provident Fund)
- ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme) — 3-year lock-in
- NSC (National Savings Certificate)
- 5-Year Tax Saving FD
- Life Insurance Premium
- EPF (Employee Provident Fund) contribution
- Tuition Fees (up to 2 children)
- Home Loan Principal Repayment
- Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana
- NPS — Additional ₹50,000 under Section 80CCD(1B)

## TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)

TDS is deducted by the employer/payer on behalf of the government. Key TDS rates:
- Salary: As per applicable slab
- Interest on FD: 10% (if interest > ₹40,000; ₹50,000 for senior citizens)
- Rent: 10% (if rent > ₹2,40,000 per year)
- Professional Fees: 10%
- Commission: 5%

PAN is mandatory. Without PAN, TDS is deducted at 20%.

## ITR Filing

ITR must be filed by July 31 of the Assessment Year. For FY 2025-26, the deadline is July 31, 2026.

ITR Forms:
- ITR-1 (Sahaj): Salary, one house property, other sources (income up to ₹50 lakh)
- ITR-2: Capital gains, multiple house properties, foreign income
- ITR-3: Business/professional income
- ITR-4 (Sugam): Presumptive business income

Late filing penalty: ₹5,000 (₹1,000 if income < ₹5 lakh). Interest of 1% per month under Section 234A.

## Capital Gains Tax

### Equity / Equity Mutual Funds
- Short-term (held < 12 months): 20%
- Long-term (held ≥ 12 months): 12.5% (exemption up to ₹1.25 lakh)

### Debt Mutual Funds (purchased after April 1, 2023)
- Taxed as per income tax slab (no indexation benefit)

### Real Estate
- Short-term (held < 24 months): As per slab
- Long-term (held ≥ 24 months): 12.5% without indexation

## Advance Tax

If total tax liability exceeds ₹10,000 in a financial year, advance tax must be paid in installments:
- By June 15: 15% of tax
- By September 15: 45% cumulative
- By December 15: 75% cumulative
- By March 15: 100%
