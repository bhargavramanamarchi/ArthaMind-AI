## What is GST?

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a comprehensive indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services in India. It replaced multiple taxes like VAT, excise duty, and service tax. GST was implemented on July 1, 2017.

## GST Structure

GST is a destination-based tax with the following components:

| Component | Full Form | When Applicable |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| CGST | Central GST | Intra-state transactions (within same state) |
| SGST | State GST | Intra-state transactions (within same state) |
| IGST | Integrated GST | Inter-state transactions (between states) |
| UTGST | Union Territory GST | Transactions within Union Territories |

For intra-state: CGST + SGST = Total GST Rate (split equally)
For inter-state: IGST = Total GST Rate

## GST Rate Slabs

| Rate | Category | Examples |
|------|----------|----------|
| 0% (Exempt) | Essential items | Fresh vegetables, fruits, milk, eggs, cereals, bread |
| 5% | Basic necessities | Packaged food items, sugar, tea, coffee, edible oil, medicines |
| 12% | Standard goods | Processed food, butter, ghee, mobile phones, sewing machines |
| 18% | Most goods & services | Biscuits, pasta, cameras, restaurants (AC), IT services, consulting |
| 28% | Luxury / Sin goods | Cars, motorcycles, aerated drinks, tobacco, cement, ACs |

## GST Registration

GST registration is mandatory if:
- Annual turnover exceeds ₹40 lakh (₹20 lakh for special category states)
- For services: turnover exceeds ₹20 lakh (₹10 lakh for special states)
- Inter-state supply of goods/services
- E-commerce operators
- Input service distributors

GSTIN: 15-digit unique identification number (format: 2-digit state code + 10-digit PAN + entity code + check digit)

## Input Tax Credit (ITC)

ITC allows businesses to reduce the tax they pay on purchases from the tax they collect on sales.

Conditions for claiming ITC:
1. Must possess a valid tax invoice
2. Goods/services must be received
3. Tax must be paid to the government by the supplier
4. Must file GSTR-3B return
5. ITC must be claimed within the time limit (November 30 of the following year or filing of annual return)

ITC is NOT available on:
- Personal use goods
- Motor vehicles (exceptions for resale, rental, transport)
- Food and beverages (unless in hospitality business)
- Membership of clubs
- Life and health insurance (exceptions apply)

## GST Returns

| Return | Purpose | Due Date |
|--------|---------|----------|
| GSTR-1 | Outward supplies | 11th of next month (monthly) / 13th (quarterly) |
| GSTR-3B | Summary return with tax payment | 20th of next month |
| GSTR-9 | Annual return | December 31 of next FY |
| GSTR-9C | Reconciliation statement | December 31 (if turnover > ₹5 crore) |

## Composition Scheme

Small taxpayers with turnover up to ₹1.5 crore (₹75 lakh for special states) can opt for the Composition Scheme.

Tax rates under Composition:
- Manufacturers: 1% (0.5% CGST + 0.5% SGST)
- Restaurants: 5% (2.5% CGST + 2.5% SGST)
- Other suppliers: 1%
- Service providers (turnover up to ₹50 lakh): 6%

Restrictions: Cannot collect tax from customers, cannot claim ITC, cannot make inter-state supplies.

## E-way Bill

E-way bill is mandatory for movement of goods worth more than ₹50,000.
- Part A: Details of goods (invoice, value, HSN code)
- Part B: Vehicle number and transport details
- Generated on: https://ewaybillgst.gov.in
- Validity: Based on distance (1 day per 200 km for regular cargo)

## HSN Codes

HSN (Harmonized System of Nomenclature) codes are used to classify goods under GST.
- Turnover up to ₹5 crore: 4-digit HSN code
- Turnover above ₹5 crore: 6-digit HSN code

Common HSN codes:
- 0401: Milk and cream
- 1006: Rice
- 6109: T-shirts
- 8517: Telephones/smartphones
- 9403: Furniture
